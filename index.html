I am a Ph.D. candidate in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Florida, advised by Prof. Swarup Bhunia at the Warren B. Nelms Institute. My research investigates the security of modern AI systems — from adversarial attacks on neural networks and large language models, to runtime detection mechanisms built directly into AI accelerator hardware.

I received my M.S. in Computer Science and Engineering from the Indian Institute of Technology Kharagpur, and have held research internships at Intel Corporation in Santa Clara and Bangalore. My work has appeared in CVPR, DAC, ATS, ISQED, the IEEE Transactions on Circuits and Systems for AI, the IEEE Transactions on VLSI Systems, and Springer Nature Computer Science.

03 / Selected Research

Things I have built.

01

SAMURAI

Runtime Attack Detection · IEEE TCSAI 2026

A framework for runtime detection of adversarial evasion attacks on AI accelerators. Couples on-chip AI Performance Counters with a lightweight ML analysis engine (TANTO) to monitor low-level hardware events during inference and flag threats — without relying on software-side defenses.

98% detection <3% energy <4% area
02

BARE-AI

Bit-Flip Attack Resilience · TVLSI (Submitted)

Real-time defense against Bit-Flip Attacks on DNN inference. Uses APCs feeding the PULSE classifier ensemble, an Activation Shift Index for layer-level fault localization, and z-score statistical repair of corrupted parameters. Evaluated on CNNs, Vision Transformers, and LLMs.

98% detection CNNs · ViTs · LLMs Fault repair
03

WINGs

Automatic Weight Generator · IEEE TC (Submitted)

A framework that dynamically generates fully connected layer weights and compresses CNN weights at inference time using PCA and lightweight SVR. Sensitivity-aware design also amplifies and exposes the impact of bit-flip attacks on compressed weights.

53× FC compression 28× AlexNet/MNIST 1–2% accuracy loss
04

DASH

Meta-Attack Framework · CVPR 2026

A differentiable, multi-stage meta-attack that composes existing ℓp-bounded attacks via learned adaptive weights to produce effective and perceptually aligned adversarial examples. A novel meta-loss jointly minimizes misclassification and perceptual distortion.

+20.63% ASR SSIM · LPIPS · FID Beats AdvAD
05

E3-CODE

Embedded ECC for Neural Networks · DAC 2026

An ECC framework that embeds multi-bit parity within the entire weight representation rather than overwriting LSBs only. Weight and parity assignments jointly optimized via MILP. Hybrid scheme combines embedded with conventional ECC for tunable resilience-overhead trade-off.

4.3× lifetime No memory overhead 10× w/ <2% mem
06

ASTRA

Distributed PUFs · IEEE TVLSI 2025

An automated framework that integrates PUF-based entropy sources into digital logic in a distributed, timing-aware fashion. Distributes memory-in-logic PUFs across the chip to maximize response randomness while minimizing area and power overhead.

Near-ideal PUF metrics DARPA AISS Synthesis-aware
