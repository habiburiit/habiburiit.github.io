Biography

Habibur Rahaman (Member, IEEE) is currently pursuing the Ph.D. degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Florida, Gainesville, FL, USA, under the guidance of Prof. Swarup Bhunia. He received the master's degree in Computer Science and Engineering from the Indian Institute of Technology Kharagpur, India.

He previously worked as a DFT Intern at Intel Corporation, Bangalore, India, from July 2021 to December 2022, and as an intern in SoC-based Security at Intel Corporation, Santa Clara, CA, USA, from August 2023 to December 2023.

His work has appeared in leading conferences and journals, including IEEE/CVF CVPR, ACM/IEEE DAC, IEEE ATS, IEEE ISQED, IEEE ISVLSI, SN Computer Science, IEEE TCASAI, and IEEE TVLSI. He was a Best Paper Award nominee at ATS for the paper "SAMURAI: Runtime Detection of Adversarial Attacks in AI Accelerators Using Performance Counters" and received the Best Demo Award for Innovative Project Demo from UF Innovate at the Warren B. Nelms Annual IoT Conference 2025 for demonstrating the SAMURAI tool.

His research interests include machine learning security, AI hardware, adversarial machine learning, hardware security, and VLSI testing.
